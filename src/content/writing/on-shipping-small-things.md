The most useful habit I have picked up in the last few years is shipping the
smallest possible version of a thing and watching what happens next.

Not "MVP" in the startup sense &mdash; that word has been overloaded into
meaninglessness. I mean the version that does *one* useful thing for *one*
person and could be deleted by Friday without anyone noticing. The version
where the README is three lines.

## Why small things win

A small thing has fewer places to hide. If users do not engage, you cannot
blame the missing feature you never built. The signal is clean.

A small thing also forces you to choose. You cannot ship every idea you
have; you have to pick the one that matters most. That decision is the
project.

## The trap

The trap is that small things look unimpressive on a resume. So we add
scope. We add a dashboard. We add a settings page. We add a fourth backend
service "for scale." None of it changes whether the thing works.

Resist.
